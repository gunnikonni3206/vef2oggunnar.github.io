The Day of the Dead, known in Spanish as El Día de Muertos or El Día de los Muertos, is a traditional Mexican holiday celebrated in honor of deceased loved ones. Taking place primarily on November 1st and 2nd, it's a time where families come together to remember and celebrate the lives of those who have passed away.

This vibrant and colorful holiday stems from ancient Aztec traditions that honored the dead and has since been merged with Catholic observance of All Saints' Day and All Souls' Day. Central to the celebration are the beautifully adorned altars, or "ofrendas", covered in marigold flowers, candles, food, and photos of the deceased. Colorful sugar skulls and skeleton figures are also popular symbols of the holiday.
